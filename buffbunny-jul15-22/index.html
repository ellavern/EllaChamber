Creative Analysis

What's Working and Why

The data tells a clear story when you look at all three metrics together.

🎬
Video Wins on Every Single Metric
The #1 ROAS creative is video - and it's not close. 23.7x ROAS, $4.44 CPA, $10.50 CPM. Compare that to the worst performer: 9.65x ROAS, $13.22 CPA, $12.42 CPM. The video format reaches people cheaper AND converts them more efficiently. That's not a coincidence.
Video #1 CPA: $4.44 vs New image bottom: $13.22
📸
One Image is Matching Video
The #2 creative is an image - 20.9x ROAS, $4.49 CPA, $9.59 CPM. Nearly identical to the video winner. This one belongs to an older creative family that's been tested and proven. It tells us the concept and creative approach is strong, not just the format.
Best image CPM in account: $9.59 - beats most videos
⚠️
New Batch is Underperforming on CPA
The new image batch (launched in the last 3 weeks) is getting the most spend but delivering the worst ROAS and CPA. The highest-spend creative at $245 has a $9.08 CPA - more than double the winner. The worst new image has a $13.22 CPA. These need a hard look before more budget gets routed there.
New image avg CPA: ~$10+ vs proven creative avg: ~$4.50
Iteration Strategy

How We're Moving Forward

Concrete next steps tied directly to what the data is showing.

1
Scale the Video Creative
The #1 ROAS creative is video at $209 spend this week. It has headroom to scale - 23.7x ROAS and $4.44 CPA means we can push more budget here and keep returns strong. Priority is increasing delivery on this asset and building 2-3 video variants off the same concept.
2
Protect the Proven Image Creative
The #2 image at 20.9x ROAS / $4.49 CPA is a proven winner that matches video performance. Keep this one running and refresh the visual before it fatigues - same concept, new execution. Don't let it die while the new batch is burning budget.
3
Put the Bottom Image on a Performance Watch
The worst performer ($106 spend, 9.65x ROAS, $13.22 CPA) needs one more week to prove itself or it gets paused. The CPA is 3x worse than the winning creative. At that efficiency gap, continuing to spend here means we're leaving revenue on the table.
4
Interrogate the Highest-Spend Creative
$245 spent on the #3 ROAS creative this week. It's profitable at 15.3x, but the $9.08 CPA versus $4.44 on the video means we're overpaying per acquisition. We're not saying pause it - we're saying the algorithm should be routing more of that budget to the top 2 performers.
Competitive Landscape

What Competitors Are Running Right Now

Top active creatives from Fabletics, Gymshark, and Oner Active ranked by impression share. All currently live.

Fabletics
High Volume / High Spend
Format strategy: Image-heavy, high volume testing. Fabletics just ran a large creative refresh in early July - all top active ads launched July 2-8. They're running multiple image variants simultaneously and leaning on clean product photography. No video in their current top set, which stands out as the rest of the category leans into video. As the spend leader in the space, they're betting on volume to find winners fast.

What this means for BuffBunny: Their approach validates running many variants - but our data shows the CPA gap between good and poor creative is enormous ($4.44 vs $13.22). Volume testing needs to come with fast kill decisions on underperformers.

Going All-In on Video
Format strategy: Video-first, running deep. Gymshark's top impression ads are 4 out of 5 video. Oner Active
Closest Comp - Video + Carousel
Format strategy: Video-led with carousel for collections - and it's working long-term. Oner is running two videos that have been live since May - 60+ days active and still in their top impression set. That level of creative longevity is rare. They're also actively testing fresh video (July launches at 1-12 days) alongside the long-runners, and using carousel format for collection showcasing that's proven out at 48-77 days.

What this means for BuffBunny: Oner's playbook is the most directly applicable. Their video + carousel combination maps well to what BuffBunny could build. Notably, we're not running carousel format at all currently - and Oner's carousels are some of their longest-running creative. Worth testing a product-collection carousel as a direct experiment.
